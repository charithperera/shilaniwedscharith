Anthony

From friend to enemy back to friends. In college our friendship was often mistaken for something a little bit more. I first met Anthony in grade five and he quickly became one of my best friends. Although he was quick to become one of my greatest enemies, in college we reconnected and he has since become one of my strangest and most elusive best friends. He’s always there for a laugh and he remains my go to source for real music.

Chow

Chow, or Chalana, is one my oldest friends from the days of Cooma. While he started of as little Chalana he quickly grew up into a fine young gentleman and one of my best friends. Late nights on MSN and hours on end of Call of Duty became our rituals. While we don’t see each other that often any more catching up is always a blast and kicking back with a glass of Chivas or Monkey Shoulder has become our new routine.

Dulshan

Dulshan will always fondly remember the night that I tried to murder him with a fork. He’s one of my oldest friends from Cooma and is one leg of the infamous tripod. From running around his garden chasing aliens as kids to countless nights out at ANU with a bottle full of passion pop counting the glowing sheep on Budjs dorm room ceiling. Hanging out with Dulshan is just great fun and you never know what he’s going to say next.

Budj

Budj was my very first friend and I distinctly remember hiding behind my mum as I was introduced to him when I was five. From there we grew up together in Cooma, sometimes holding hands at school and being teased about it. As the years went by we moved apart but we continued to spend the holidays together. Playing video games, getting caught by the police, and getting up to mischief is how we spent them. He is now the third leg in our infamous tripod and he remains one of my best and closest friends.

Alex

Alex is one of my oldest high school friends and is renowned for having sold me a ‘genuine’ copy of Star Craft for a nice profit. That’s ok though because for almost two years I managed to sell my mums curry chicken sandwiches to him for $2 a pop giving me access to Sausage Rolls and Meat Pies. Alex is one of the nicest and most genuine guys I’ve ever met. It’s an absolute pleasure seeing him and his family every time I’m in Canberra.

Kosal

Everyone will agree that Kosal is one the most elusive people in the world. I met him in my early days at ANU and we bonded over our passion for breakdancing. Ultimately this passion led nowhere but I’ll always remember our Saturdays trying to master backflips while Anthony went to the gym to lift weights. As elusive as he is he will always be my friend that can turn a dull night into a party just by showing up.

Dino

Dino and I bonded over countless emails at work about lifting weights, our favourite Battlefield stories and…I don’t even remember anymore. We would end the week religiously with Friday night at Tongue & Groove and Saturday night at Monkey Bar chasing the best old school we could find in Canberra. This was our routine and we stuck to it like a vigorous weight lifting routine. Times have certainly changed but he remains one of the funniest and best friends I have.

Getting Around

There’s a few ways to get around Brisbane. These include bus, ferry, train, taxis and Uber. If you want to use the bus, ferry or train system you’ll need to obtain a Go Card. The easiest place to purchase these is at any 7-Eleven, select Newsagents or a Railway station. Our top pick for getting around is definitely Uber. If you’re already on the Uber bandwagon you’ll have no issues getting around Brisbane.

Accomodation

There’s plenty to see in Brisbane Australia. Exploring South Bank was one of our favourites as it has some great spots for food and entertainment. If you feel like going for a drive we recommend Mount Coot-tha Botanic Gardens to enjoy Brisbane from above. There’s Queen Street Mall to get your shopping fix and if you get a chance jump on a CityCat ferry to see Brisbane River.

Attractions

Any Hotel within the CBD will provide you with a short Uber or Taxi ride to Victoria Park. Some of the closer hotels include Quest in Kelvin Grove, Mantra Richmont, and Hotel Grand Chancellor. Don’t be afraid to explore some of the nicer offerings in the city though as Victoria Park is just a stones throw away. We have more information about this in our infosheet available for download below.

What to Wear

Brisbane is hot and there’s no way around that. You’ll be coming in Spring though so it won’t be too bad. Temperatures will be between 25 to 30 with a bit of humidity. There is however a chance of rain, and when it rains in Brisbane it pours. We suggest you pack an assortment of attire but mostly cater for a warm and somewhat humid weekend.
